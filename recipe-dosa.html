Crispy Masala Dosa

Makes 8–10 dosas · Prep 8 hr (soaking) · Cook 25 min

Thin, crispy fermented rice-and-lentil crepes filled with spiced potato masala. A South Indian breakfast classic.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup short-grain rice, ¼ cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 2 tbsp flattened rice (poha), soaked with rice and dal 6–8 hours
  • Salt to taste, water for grinding
  • For masala: 3–4 potatoes, 1 onion, mustard seeds, curry leaves, turmeric, salt
  • Oil or ghee for cooking dosas

Instructions

  1. Drain the soaked rice, urad dal, and poha. Grind to a smooth batter with enough water to make a pourable consistency. Add salt and ferment overnight or 8–12 hours in a warm place until slightly puffed and tangy.
  2. Boil, peel, and mash the potatoes. Sauté mustard seeds, curry leaves, onion, turmeric, and salt; mix in the mashed potatoes for the masala filling.
  3. Heat a nonstick or cast-iron pan. Pour a ladle of batter and spread in a thin circle. Drizzle oil around the edges. When the bottom is golden and crisp, place some potato masala in the center and fold the dosa. Serve hot with chutney and sambar.
