Homemade Margherita Pizza

Makes 2 pizzas · Prep 20 min + rise · Cook 12 min

Classic Margherita pizza with a simple tomato base, fresh mozzarella, and basil. Perfect for a weekend project or pizza night.

Ingredients

  • 2½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp instant yeast
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ cup tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Salt and olive oil for drizzling

Instructions

  1. Mix flour, yeast, and salt in a bowl. Add warm water and olive oil; stir until a shaggy dough forms. Knead on a floured surface for 5–7 minutes until smooth. Place in an oiled bowl, cover, and let rise until doubled, about 1 hour.
  2. Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C) with a pizza stone or baking sheet inside. Divide dough in half and roll or stretch each into a 12-inch round.
  3. Place one round on a floured peel or inverted baking sheet. Spread half the tomato sauce, then half the mozzarella. Slide onto the hot stone and bake until crust is golden and cheese is melted, about 10–12 minutes. Top with basil and a drizzle of olive oil. Repeat with the second pizza.
