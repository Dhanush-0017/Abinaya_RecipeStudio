Hyderabad Chicken Dum Biryani

Serves 6 · Prep 45 min · Cook 1 hr 20 min (including dum)

Layered basmati rice and marinated chicken cooked in the dum style—sealed and slow-cooked so the aromas stay in. A Hyderabad classic with saffron, mint, and fried onions.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked 30 min
  • 1 lb chicken (thighs or drumsticks), cut into pieces
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced and fried until golden
  • 1 cup yogurt, 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp biryani masala or homemade mix (cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, mace)
  • 1 tsp red chili powder, ½ tsp turmeric, 1 tsp salt
  • Fresh mint and cilantro, chopped
  • Pinch of saffron soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk
  • 2 tbsp ghee, 4 cups water for par-boiling rice
  • Lemon juice, rose water (optional)

Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, biryani masala, chili powder, turmeric, salt, half the fried onions, and half the mint and cilantro. Let sit 30 minutes to 2 hours.
  2. Par-boil the soaked, drained rice in salted water until 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a heavy pot, layer: half the rice, then the marinated chicken, then remaining rice. Top with remaining fried onions, mint, cilantro, saffron milk, ghee, and a drizzle of rose water if using. Seal the pot with a tight lid or dough so steam doesn’t escape (dum).
  4. Cook on very low heat for 35–45 minutes. Rest 10 minutes, then open and fluff gently. Serve with raita and salad.
